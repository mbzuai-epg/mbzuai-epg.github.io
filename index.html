Ivan Laptev

The Robotics Perception Group at MBZUAI, led by Prof. Ivan Laptev, advances the frontiers of visual perception, human action understanding, and robotic intelligence. The group’s research tackles core challenges in human-object interaction, scene understanding, and temporal recognition across video and real-world sensory data. Current efforts are directed toward enabling machines to perceive, interpret, and act upon complex visual stimuli, with applications spanning autonomous systems, human-robot collaboration, and embodied AI. The group bridges fundamental research in computer vision with practical, deployable systems in robotics.

Prof. Ivan Laptev is a Professor of Computer Vision and Robotics at MBZUAI and Head of Research at VisionLabs. He is currently on leave from INRIA Paris, where he has long been a senior researcher. Ivan holds a PhD in Computer Science from the Royal Institute of Technology (KTH), awarded in 2004, and a Habilitation degree from École Normale Supérieure (ENS), completed in 2013.

With a career spanning over two decades, Prof. Laptev has held key academic and industrial positions, building bridges between foundational research and applied AI. He has trained and mentored numerous researchers through INRIA summer schools and international conferences, playing a vital role in shaping the next generation of computer vision experts.

Prof. Laptev has authored over 150 peer-reviewed papers, with publications in top-tier venues such as CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, and IJCV. His work has made influential contributions to the understanding of visual dynamics and action recognition.
He has served as Associate Editor for both International Journal of Computer Vision (IJCV) and IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI). He was Program Chair for CVPR 2018 and ICCV 2023, and will chair ACCV 2024. He is a frequent Area Chair at major vision conferences including CVPR, ICCV, and ECCV.

Prof. Laptev has co-organized numerous workshops, tutorials, and challenges in vision and robotics, including the renowned Machines Can See summits (2017–2023) and a series of INRIA summer schools on Computer Vision and Machine Learning (2010–2013).
His work has been recognized with prestigious awards including the ERC Starting Grant (2012) and the Helmholtz Prize (2017).
